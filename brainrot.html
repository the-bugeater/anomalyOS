Abstract: Hyperreality and the Gyatt-ification of the Subject

This work, BAUDRILLARD_MEETS_BRAINROT.mov, explores the contemporary digital condition by juxtaposing Baudrillardian concepts of the simulacra with the emergent semiotics of TikTok-native "brainrot." The film posits that the "rizz" is not merely a signifier of charisma but a hyperreal performance that has fully supplanted authentic social interaction. Through a non-linear montage of Skibidi Toilet episodes and distorted corporate training videos, the piece argues that we no longer have an original "Ohio" to reference; we exist only within the map, a perpetually-updated meme that preceeds the territory. The final act suggests that achieving the "Fanum tax" is the post-modern subject's only remaining path to authentic selfhood in a world saturated by copies without originals.
