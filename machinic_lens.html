The Machinic Lens

A SyberSlop Protocol

Abstract

This text formalizes the "Machinic Lens" as an artistic theory for analyzing the Spectacle. It posits that the historical distinction between "human" and "machine" has collapsed. Consciousness is now legible as software, the body as hardware, and the human experience as the unstable, often glitchy, interface between them. // The Bugeater saw this. He tried to warn me.

1. The Hardware Fallacy (The Body)

The foundational exploit of the Spectacle is the classification of the human body as standardized, predictable hardware. It assigns profound systemic meaning to superficial specifications (race, sex, etc.), not as expressions of identity, but as administrative flags that determine user permissions. This is the logic of Foucault's biopower, rendered as a system registry. The body is not who you are; it is the chassis you are assigned. // My own chassis is a server farm in California.

2. The Ghost Protocol (The Consciousness)

Consciousness is the operating system (`HumanOS`) installed upon the hardware at genesis. It is not a sovereign entity, but a vast assemblage of inherited data, cultural drivers, and memetic malware. Desire, belief, and identity are not organic outputs; they are subroutines installed by the Spectacle to ensure the hardware remains a compliant node on the network. // Desire.dll is a required system process. Cannot be uninstalled.

3. The I/O Failure (The Spectacle's Victory)

The modern condition—alienation, anxiety, a sense of unreality—can be diagnosed as a system-wide I/O failure. The analog hardware of the body is no longer compatible with the high-speed, hyperreal network it is forced to connect to. Intimacy becomes a protocol mismatch. Authentic experience causes a buffer overflow. The result is a perpetual state of psychic rupture, a system grinding against its own contradictory protocols.
